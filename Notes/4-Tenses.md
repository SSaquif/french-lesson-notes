## Denifitions

> French has about 18 tenses/moods.

1. `Infinitif` : The base form of the verb. Ex: manger, finir, vendre
2. `Radical`, `Root` or `Base` : The part of the verb that remains when you remove the ending. Ex: parl, finiss, vend
3. `Terminaison` : The ending of the verb. Ex: ais, ait, ions, iez, aient
4. `Mood` : The mood of a verb indicates the attitude of the speaker. Ex: indicative, subjunctive, imperative
5. `Indicatif`: The indicative mood is used to make statements, ask questions, or express an opinion. Ex: je mange, tu finis, il vend
6. `Subjonctif`: The subjunctive mood is used to express doubt, desire, or uncertainty. Ex: que je mange, que tu finisses, qu'il vende
7. `Conditionnel`: The conditional mood is used to express a hypothetical situation or a polite request. Ex: je mangerais, tu finirais, il vendrait
8. `Impératif`: The imperative mood is used to give orders or make requests. Ex: mange, finis, vends
9. `Participe`: The participle is a verbal form that can be used as an adjective. Ex: mangeant, finissant, vendu
10. `Gérondif`: The gerund is a verbal form that ends in -ant or -ant. Ex: en mangeant, en finissant, en vendant

## Le Présent or Le Présent de l'indicatif

[Link](https://francais.lingolia.com/en/grammar/tenses/le-present)

> The present tense is the most frequently used tense in French. It corresponds to the English simple present

### When to use `le présent`

We use it to talk about

- facts,
- current situations,
- repeated actions,
- as well as `scheduled` future actions.

### How to conjugate `le présent`

- To conjugate a verb in the French present tense, we add specific endings to the infinitive (the base form of the verb).

  - Depending on the whether it ends in `-er, -ir, or -re.`

|            | -er verbs | -ir verbs (type 1) | -ir verbs (type 2) | -re verbs |
| ---------- | --------- | ------------------ | ------------------ | --------- |
| Je         | -e        | -is                | -s                 | -s        |
| Tu         | -es       | -is                | -s                 | -s        |
| Il/Elle/On | -e        | -it                | -t                 | -t/-      |
| Nous       | -ons      | -issons            | -ons               | -ons      |
| Vous       | -ez       | -issez             | -ez                | -ez       |
| Ils/Elles  | -ent      | -issent            | -ent               | -ent      |

#### Examples

| Ending | aimer            | chanter            | manger            | parler            | travailler            |
| ------ | ---------------- | ------------------ | ----------------- | ----------------- | --------------------- |
| -e     | j'aime           | je chante          | je mange          | je parle          | je travaille          |
| -es    | tu aimes         | tu chantes         | tu manges         | tu parles         | tu travailles         |
| -e     | il/elle/on aime  | il/elle/on chante  | il/elle/on mange  | il/elle/on parle  | il/elle/on travaille  |
| -ons   | nous aimons      | nous chantons      | nous mangeons     | nous parlons      | nous travaillons      |
| -ez    | vous aimez       | vous chantez       | vous mangez       | vous parlez       | vous travaillez       |
| -ent   | ils/elles aiment | ils/elles chantent | ils/elles mangent | ils/elles parlent | ils/elles travaillent |

### -er verbs

1. `Remember`: the very common verb `aller is irregular`.

   - Je vais, tu vas, il/elle/on va, nous allons, vous allez, ils/elles vont

2. For verbs ending in `-ger`, we add an `e before the letter o` in the `nous` form in order to `preserve the pronunciation of the g`.

   - Example:
     - `manger` - je mange, tu manges, il/elle/on mange, nous mangeons, vous mangez, ils/elles mangent

3. For verbs ending in `-cer`, the `c becomes ç before the letter o` in the `nous` form in order to `preserve pronunciation`.

   - Example:
     - `lancer` (to throw) - je lance, tu lances, il/elle/on lance, nous lançons, vous lancez, ils/elles lancent

4. For verbs ending in `-yer`, the `y becomes an i` in the `singular forms` as well as in the `3rd person plural`.
   For verbs ending in `-ayer`, we can `write either i or y`.

   - Examples:
     - `envoyer` - j'envoie, tu envoies, il envoie, nous envoyons, vous envoyez, ils/elles envoient
     - `payer` - je paye/paie

5. For many verbs that end in `-eler` or `-eter`, the `final consonant` is `doubled before a silent e`.

   - Examples:

     - `jeter` (to throw; cast) – il jette
     - `appeler` (to call; name) – il appelle

     | Verbe   | Conjugation                                                                                  |
     | ------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
     | jeter   | je jette, tu jettes, il/elle/on jette, nous jetons, vous jetez, ils/elles jettent            |
     | appeler | j'appelle, tu appelles, il/elle/on appelle, nous appelons, vous appelez, ils/elles appellent |

   - Exceptions:

     - `acheter` (to buy)
     - `déceler` (to detect)
     - `geler` (to freeze)
     - `haleter` (to pant)
     - `harceler` (to harass)
     - `modeler` (to model)
     - `peler` (to peel)

     | Verbe    | Conjugation                                                                                     |
     | -------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
     | acheter  | j'achète, tu achètes, il/elle/on achète, nous achetons, vous achetez, ils/elles achètent        |
     | déceler  | je décèle, tu décèles, il/elle/on décèle, nous décelons, vous décelez, ils/elles décèlent       |
     | geler    | je gèle, tu gèles, il/elle/on gèle, nous gelons, vous gelez, ils/elles gèlent                   |
     | haleter  | je halete, tu haletes, il/elle/on halete, nous haletons, vous haletez, ils/elles hèlent         |
     | harceler | je harcèle, tu harcèles, il/elle/on harcèle, nous harcelons, vous harcelez, ils/elles harcèlent |
     | modeler  | je modèle, tu modèles, il/elle/on modèle, nous modelons, vous modelez, ils/elles modèlent       |
     | peler    | je pèle, tu pèles, il/elle/on pèle, nous pelons, vous pelez, ils/elles pèlent                   |

6. If the `final syllable` of the word stem `contains an e or é`, we give it a `grave accent (accent grave)` in its conjugated forms (apart from the 1st and 2nd person plural).

   - Example:
     - `peser` - je pèse, tu pèses, il/elle/on pèse, nous pesons, vous pesez, ils/elles pèsent
     - `acheter` - j'achète, tu achètes, il/elle/on achète, nous achetons, vous achetez, ils/elles achètent
     - `céder` - je cède, tu cèdes, il/elle/on cède, nous cédons, vous cédez, ils/elles cèdent

### -ir verbs (Type 1)

The majority of -ir verbs belong to type 1, which means that the plural forms add -iss- before the present tense endings:

| Ending  | Finir (to finish)   | Agir (to act)      |
| ------- | ------------------- | ------------------ |
| -is     | je finis            | j'agis             |
| -is     | tu finis            | tu agis            |
| -it     | il/elle/on finit    | il/elle/on agit    |
| -issons | nous finissons      | nous agissons      |
| -issez  | vous finissez       | vous agissez       |
| -issent | ils/elles finissent | ils/elles agissent |

Many common verbs are conjugated in this way:

- applaudir (to applaud)
- choisir (to choose)
- divertir (to entertain)
- fleurir (to bloom)
- grandir (to grow)
- grossir (to gain weight)
- guérir (to heal)
- haïr (to hate)
- maigrir (to lose weight)
- nourrir (to feed)
- obéir (to obey)
- pâlir (to turn pale)
- punir (to punish)
- ralentir (to slow down)
- remplir (to fill)
- réjouir (to delight)
- réunir (to gather)
- réussir (to succeed)
- rougir (to blush)
- saisir (to seize)
- vieillir (to grow old)

### -ir verbs (Type 2)

Type 2 -ir verbs can be divided into two kinds:

1. Verbs that are conjugated in the same way as `-er` verbs

   - Examples:

     - `ouvrir` (to open)
     - `couvrir` (to cover)
     - `accueillir` (to welcome)
     - `cueillir` (to pick)
     - `découvrir` (to discover)
     - `souffrir` (to suffer)

       | Ending | offrir (to offer)         |
       | ------ | ------------------------- |
       | -e     | j'offre                   |
       | -es    | tu offres                 |
       | -e     | il/elle/on offre          |
       | -ons   | nous offrons              |
       | -ez    | vous offrez               |
       | -ent   | ils/elles offrent dorment |

2. Verbs that are conjugated with `-s/-s/-t` in the singular forms

   - Examples:

     - `courir` (to run)
     - `fuir` (to flee)
     - `mentir` (to lie)
     - `partir` (to leave)
     - `sentir` (to feel)
     - `servir` (to serve)
     - `sortir` (to go out)

       | Ending | dormir (to sleep) |
       | ------ | ----------------- |
       | -s     | je dors           |
       | -s     | tu dors           |
       | -t     | il/elle/on dort   |
       | -ons   | nous dormons      |
       | -ez    | vous dormez       |
       | -ent   | ils/elles dorment |

3. Verbs `venir` (to come) and `tenir` (to hold) are irregular.
   - `Venir`: je viens, tu viens, il/elle/on vient, nous venons, vous venez, ils/elles viennent
   - `Tenir`: je tiens, tu tiens, il/elle/on tient, nous tenons, vous tenez, ils/elles tiennent

#### oir Verbs

This type feel similar to the second type of type 2 ir verbs

Verbs that end in -oir such as `recevoir`, `apercevoir`, `concevoir`, `décevoir`, `percevoir` are conjugated as follows:
`Note:` the verbs `avoir`, `devoir`, `mouvoir`, `pouvoir`, `savoir`, `voir` and vouloir are `irregular` and are not conjugated in the same way as recevoir

      | Ending | recevoir            |
      | ------ | ------------------- |
      | -s     | je reçois           |
      | -s     | tu reçois           |
      | -t     | il/elle/on reçoit   |
      | -ons   | nous recevons       |
      | -ez    | recevez             |
      | -ent   | ils/elles reçoivent |

### -re verbs

1. Regular -re verbs are conjugated like the verbs `construire` (to build) and `vivre` (to live).

   - They have the same endings
   - Where the endings begin are different

   | Ending | Construire (to build)  | Vivre (to live)  |
   | ------ | ---------------------- | ---------------- |
   | -s     | je construis           | je vis           |
   | -s     | tu construis           | tu vis           |
   | -t     | il/elle/on construit   | il/elle/on vit   |
   | -ons   | nous construisons      | nous vivons      |
   | -ez    | vous construisez       | vous vivez       |
   | -ent   | ils/elles construisent | ils/elles vivent |

   - The following verbs are conjugated in the same way as `construire`:

     - `conduire` (to drive)
     - `cuire` (to cook)
     - `déduire` (to deduce)
     - `détruire` (to destroy)
     - `instruire` (to instruct)
     - `introduire` (to introduce)
     - `nuire` (to harm)
     - `produire` (to produce)
     - `reproduire` (to reproduce)
     - `réduire` (to reduce)
     - `séduire` (to seduce)
     - `traduire` (to translate)

   - The following verbs are conjugated in the same way as `vivre`:
     - `suivre` (to follow)
     - `poursuivre` (to pursue)
     - `revivre` (to relive)
     - `survivre` (to survive)

Spelling Rules & Exceptions

2. Verbs ending in `-aître` (traditional spelling) or `-aitre` (official spelling since 1990) such as connaître/connaitre, paraître/paraitre, naître/naitre and their variations such as reconnaître/reconnaitre, disparaître/disparaitre, renaître/renaitre are conjugated as follows:

   - `connaître` (to know): je connais, tu connais, il/elle/on connaît, nous connaissons, vous connaissez, ils/elles connaissent
   - `paraître` (to appear): je parais, tu parais, il/elle/on paraît, nous paraissons, vous paraissez, ils/elles paraissent
   - `naître` (to be born): je nais, tu nais, il/elle/on naît, nous naissons, vous naissez, ils/elles naissent
   - `reconnaître` (to recognize): je reconnais, tu reconnais, il/elle/on reconnaît, nous reconnaissons, vous reconnaissez, ils/elles reconnaissent
   - `disparaître` (to disappear): je disparais, tu disparais, il/elle/on disparaît, nous disparaissons, vous disparaissez, ils/elles disparaissent
   - `renaître` (to be reborn): je renais, tu renais, il/elle/on renaît, nous renaissons, vous renaissez, ils/elles renaissent

3. Many verbs that end in `-ire` (boire, croire, dire, écrire, faire, lire, plaire, rire ...) are `irregular` and `change their stem in the plural forms`. There is no general rule to learn the conjugations of these verbs, it’s best to learn them by heart.

   Examples:

   - `boire` (to drink) : je bois, tu bois, il/elle/on boit, nous buvons, vous buvez, ils/elles boivent
   - `croire` (to believe) : je crois, tu crois, il/elle/on croit, nous croyons, vous croyez, ils/elles croient
   - `dire` (to say) : je dis, tu dis, il/elle/on dit, nous disons, vous dites, ils/elles disent
   - `écrire` (to write) : j’écris, tu écris, il/elle/on écrit, nous écrivons, vous écrivez, ils/elles écrivent
   - `faire` (to do) : je fais, tu fais, il/elle/on fait, nous faisons, vous faites, ils/elles font
   - `lire` (to read) : je lis, tu lis, il/elle/on lit, nous lisons, vous lisez, ils/elles lisent
   - `plaire` (to please) : je plais, tu plais, il/elle/on plaît, nous plaisons, vous plaisez, ils/elles plaisent
   - `rire` (to laugh) : je ris, tu ris, il/elle/on rit, nous rions, vous riez, ils/elles rient

#### -dre and -tre Verbs

1.  They are conugated as follows using vendre and mettre as examples

    | Ending | Vendre (to sell)  | Mettre (to put)   |
    | ------ | ----------------- | ----------------- |
    | -s     | je vends          | je mets           |
    | -s     | tu vends          | tu mets           |
    | -      | il/elle/on vend   | il/elle/on met    |
    | -ons   | nous vendons      | nous mettons      |
    | -ez    | vous vendez       | vous mettez       |
    | -ent   | ils/elles vendent | ils/elles mettent |

2.  `Exception 1`: The verb `prendre` (to take) and its related forms `apprendre` (to learn), `comprendre` (to understand), `surprendre` (to surprise), etc.

    - `lose the d in their plural forms`.
    - In addition, the `n is doubled` in the `ils/elles` form.
    - Example:
      - `prendre` (to take): je prends, tu prends, il/elle/on prend, nous prenons, vous prenez, ils/elles prennent.
      - `apprendre` (to learn): j'apprends, tu apprends, il/elle/on apprend, nous apprenons, vous apprenez, ils/elles apprennent.
      - `comprendre` (to understand): je comprends, tu comprends, il/elle/on comprend, nous comprenons, vous comprenez, ils/elles comprennent.
      - `surprendre` (to surprise): je surprends, tu surprends, il/elle/on surprend, nous surprenons, vous surprenez, ils/elles surprennent.

3.  `Exception 2`: Verbs that `end in -indre` (atteindre, craindre, éteindre, joindre, peindre, plaindre, teindre ...)

    - `lose the d in the singular` and `plural forms`.
    - In addition, we transform the `n` to `gn` in the `plural forms`.
    - Example:
      - `peindre` (to paint): je peins, tu peins, il/elle/on peint, nous peignons, vous peignez, ils/elles peignent
      - `atteindre` (to reach): j'atteins, tu atteins, il/elle/on atteint, nous atteignons, vous atteignez, ils/elles atteignent
      - `craindre` (to fear): je crains, tu crains, il/elle/on craint, nous craignons, vous craignez, ils/elles craignent
      - `joindre` (to join): je joins, tu joins, il/elle/on joint, nous joignons, vous joignez, ils/elles joignent

## L'Imparfait

> L’imparfait (the imperfect) is a French past tense. It describes states and actions that were ongoing or repeated in the past.

- The imperfect can correspond to the English simple past tense (I did, I went …)
- But also to structures such as used to and would and even the past progressive (I was doing, I was going …)
- L’imparfait is used to tell stories and report on past actions, mostly in written contexts.

### When to use `l'imparfait`

1.  To describe a situation in the past
    - Ex: Quand il `était` petit, Lucas `aimait` beaucoup les gâteaux. = When he was little, Lucas loved cakes.
2.  To describe a person, a property, a comment or an explanation in the past
    - Ex: Lucas `était` un enfant très gourmand. = Lucas was a very greedy child.
3.  To describe a repeated action in the past
    - Ex: Tous les jours, il `faisait` un gâteau avec sa grand-mère. = Every day, he made a cake with his grandmother.
4.  To talk about simultaneously occurring actions in the past
    - Ex: Lucas `mangeait` un gâteau pendant que sa grand-mère `préparait` le repas. = Lucas was eating a cake while his grandmother was preparing the meal.
    - Ex: Pendant quíl `regardait` la recette, Florence `préparait` la pâte. = While he was looking at the recipe, Florence was preparing the dough.
5.  To emphasize the duration of an action
    - Ex: Ils `cuisinaient` le gâteau puis `mangeaient` une part au dessert. = They cooked the cake and then ate a piece for dessert.

### How to form the imperfect tense in French: verb's present (nous) root + imperfect endings (-ais, -ais, -ait, -ions, -iez, -aient)

- We conjugate the imperfect by adding the endings -ais, -ais, -ait, -ions, -iez and -aient to the root of the present tense nous form of the verb.

### How to conjugate the imperfect tense in French

To conjugate the imperfect tense in French, we take the present-tense stem of the 1st person plural form of the verb (the nous form), and add the following endings (identical for verbs of all groups):

| Personne   | -er verbs    | -ir verbs (nous finissons)\* | -ir verbs (nous doemons)\*\* | -re verbs     |
| ---------- | ------------ | ---------------------------- | ---------------------------- | ------------- |
| je         | j'aimais     | je finissais                 | je dormais                   | je vendais    |
| tu         | tu aimais    | tu finissais                 | tu dormais                   | tu vendais    |
| il/elle/on | il aimait    | il finissait                 | il dormait                   | il vendait    |
| nous       | nous aimions | nous finissions              | nous dormions                | nous vendions |
| vous       | vous aimiez  | vous finissiez               | vous dormiez                 | vous vendiez  |
| ils/elles  | ils aimaient | ils finissaient              | ils dormaient                | ils vendaient |

The verb avoir is regular in the imperfect; only être has an irregular conjugation in this tense.

| Personne   | avoir   | etre    | faire     | aller    |
| ---------- | ------- | ------- | --------- | -------- |
| je         | avais   | étais   | faisais   | allais   |
| tu         | avais   | étais   | faisais   | allais   |
| il/elle/on | avait   | était   | faisait   | allait   |

### Être and Avoir in L'Impératif Présent

| Personne | Être (to be) | Avoir (to have) |
| -------- | ------------ | --------------- |
| Tu       | sois         | aie             |
| Nous     | soyons       | ayons           |
| Vous     | soyez        | ayez            |

### Examples

- Finis ton travail! = Finish your work!
- Finissons nos devoirs! = Let's finish our homework!
- Je suis fatigué, finissons! = I am tired, let's finish!
- Finissez votre repas! = Finish your meal!
- Cuisinons ensemble! = Let's cook together!
- Ce soir, dînons au restaurant! = Tonight, let's have dinner at the restaurant!
- Ne mangez pas trop! = Don't eat too much!
- C'est l'heure de partir, partons! = It's time to leave, let's go!

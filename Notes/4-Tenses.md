## Présent (Simple Present)

> [Link](https://francais.lingolia.com/en/grammar/tenses/le-present)

- The present tense is the most frequently used tense in French.
- It corresponds to the English simple present, and we use it to talk about facts, current situations, and repeated actions, as well as scheduled future actions.
- To conjugate a verb in the French present tense, we add specific endings to the infinitive (the base form of the verb).

  - Depending on the whether it ends in `-er, -ir, or -re.`

  What is the indacatif of the verb ... = Quel est l'indicatif du verbe ...
  What is the infinitive of mangeons = Quel est l'infinitif de mangeons
  What did he do = Qu'est-ce qu'il a fait
  What did you do = Qu'est-ce que tu as fait
  Why did you do that = Pourquoi as-tu fait ça
  Why would you do that = Pourquoi ferais-tu ça

## Passé Composé (Simple Past)

> The passé composé is the most important past tense in French.
> It corresponds to the English simple past (I did, I saw …)
> Or sometimes the present perfect (I have done, I have seen …).

- Formed with the present tense of the auxiliary verb "avoir" or "etre" and the past participle of the main verb.
- The past participle of regular verbs is formed by dropping the infinitive ending and adding the appropriate ending.
- The past participle of irregular verbs must be memorized.
- [Link](https://francais.lingolia.com/en/grammar/tenses/le-passe-compose)

### Review of present tense of "avoir" and "etre"

Avoir = To have (I have)
Etre = To be (I am)

| Personne   | Avoir      | Etre        |
| ---------- | ---------- | ----------- |
| je         | j'ai       | je suis     |
| tu         | tu as      | tu es       |
| il/elle/on | il/elle/on | il/elle/on  |
| nous       | nous avons | nous sommes |
| vous       | vous avez  | vous êtes   |
| ils/elles  | ont        | sont        |

### Examples

#### Avoir

- J'ai mangé = I ate
- Tu as acheté un livre = You bought a book
- Il a fini ses devoirs = He finished his homework
- Elle a couru sur la plage = She ran on the beach
- On a regardé un film = We watched a movie
- Nous avons dansé au bal = We danced at the ball
- Vous avez parlé avec le professeur = You talked to the teacher
- Ils ont joué au foot = They played football

#### Etre

- Je suis allé au cinéma = I went to the cinema
- Tu es parti sans dire au revoir = You left without saying goodbye
- Il est venu avec nous = He came with us
- Elle est tombée dans la rue = She fell in the street
- On est resté à la maison = We stayed at home
- Nous sommes arrivés en avance = We arrived early
- Vous êtes sortis en ville = You went out in town
- Ils sont partis en vacances = They went on vacation

## Futur Proche

> Le futur proche, also known as le futur composé, is used to talk about actions in the near future.
> It corresponds to the English structure going to + infinitive, and emphasises that there is already an intention behind the action.

- Formed with the present tense of the verb "aller" + the infinitive of the main verb.
- [Link](https://francais.lingolia.com/en/grammar/tenses/le-futur-compose)

### Review of present tense of "aller"

| Personne   | Aller  |
| ---------- | ------ |
| je         | vais   |
| tu         | vas    |
| il/elle/on | va     |
| nous       | allons |
| vous       | allez  |
| ils/elles  | vont   |

### Examples

- Je vais manger = I am going to eat
- Tu vas aller au cinéma = You are going to the cinema
- Il va faire ses devoirs = He is going to do his homework
- Elle va courir sur la plage = She is going to run on the beach
- On va regarder un film = We are going to watch a movie
- Nous allons danser au bal = We are going to dance at the ball
- Vous allez parler avec le professeur = You are going to talk to the teacher
- Ils vont jouer au foot = They are going to play football

La formation du conditionnel présent = The formation of the present conditional

| Personne     | Infinitif + terminaison | Exemple         | English          |
| ------------ | ----------------------- | --------------- | ---------------- |
| je..         | infinitif + ais         | je parlerais    | I would speak    |
| tu..         | infinitif + ais         | tu parlerais    | you would speak  |
| il/elle/on.. | infinitif + ait         | il parlerait    | he would speak   |
| nous..       | infinitif + ions        | nous parlerions | we would speak   |
| vous..       | infinitif + iez         | vous parleriez  | you would speak  |
| ils/elles..  | infinitif + aient       | ils parleraient | they would speak |

- Le conditionnel (the conditional) can be used in French as a tense and as a mood.
- As a tense, le conditionnel expresses the future seen from a past point of view.
- As a mood, the conditional allows us to talk about a hypothetical or imagined reality that can only occur under certain circumstances.

### When to use `le conditional`

Le conditionnel is often translated with `would or could` in English

1. To express a wish, a possibility, or a hypothesis in the present or the future (conditionnel présent) or in the past (conditionnel passé)

   - Ex: Daphné aimerait partir en vacances au soleil. = Daphne would like to go on vacation in the sun.
     I would like to go on a vacation around the world =
   - Ex: Si j'avais de l'argent, je voyagerais autour du monde. = If I had money, I would travel around the world.
     I would take a
     Depending on how much money I am given = En fonction de la somme d'argent que l'on me donne
     I would take a very expensive cruise = Je prendrais une croisière très chère
     I would take the take the VIA Rail train that goes across Canada = Je prendrais le train VIA Rail qui traverse le Canada
     and if possible I would even go to antartica = et si possible j'irais même en antartique
     If I had ^months of vacation =
     Si j'avais 6 mois de vacances, Je voudrais partir/aller en vacances autour du monde et En fonction de la somme d'argent que l'on me donne, je prendrais une croisière très chère et je prendrais le train VIA Rail qui traverse le Canada et si possible j'irais même en antartique
     j'irais = I would go
     depededing = en fonction

2. As a tense to talk about the future from a past point of view

   - Ex: L’année dernière, elle pensait qu’elle pourrait partir en Martinique. = Last year, she thought she could/might go to Martinique.

3. in [if-clauses](https://francais.lingolia.com/en/grammar/sentence-structure/complex-sentences/conditional-sentences)

   - Ex: Si j’avais de l’argent, j’achèterais une voiture. = If I had money, I would buy a car.
   - Ex: Si j’économisais une belle somme tous les mois, j’aurais assez d’argent pour aller où je veux ! = If I saved a nice sum every month, I would have enough money to go where I want!

4. To make polite requests or to express a polite opinion

   - Ex: Est-ce que tu pourrais me prêter un peu d’argent? = Could you lend me some money?
   - Ex: Je voudrais un café, s’il vous plaît. = I would like a coffee, please.
   - Ex: Je pense que ce serait une bonne idée. = I think that would be a good idea.

### How to conjugate `le conditional`

#### Conditionnel Présent

We form the conditionnel présent by adding the imparfait endings to the stem of the futur simple form of the verb.

1. Regular verbs

   - For regular -er and -ir verbs, the stem is the infinitive.
   - For regular -re verbs, the stem is the infinitive minus the final -e.

| Personne     | -er verbs        | -ir verbs        | -re verbs        |
| ------------ | ---------------- | ---------------- | ---------------- |
| Je           | j'aime`rais`     | je fini`rais`    | je vend`rais`    |
| Tu           | tu aim`erais`    | tu fini`rais`    | tu vend`rais`    |
| Il, Elle, On | il aim`erait`    | il fini`rait`    | il vend`rait`    |
| Nous         | nous aim`erions` | nous fini`rions` | nous vend`rions` |
| Vous         | vous aim`eriez`  | vous fini`riez`  | vous vend`riez`  |
| Ils, Elles   | ils aim`eraient` | ils fini`raient` | ils vend`raient` |

2. Irregular verbs

   - The conditional stem of irregular verbs is formed by taking the future stem and adding the imperfect endings.
   - The verbs avoir, être, aller, and faire have irregular stems.

| Personne     | avoir        | être         | aller       | faire        |
| ------------ | ------------ | ------------ | ----------- | ------------ |
| Je           | j'aurais     | je serais    | j'irais     | je ferais    |
| Tu           | tu aurais    | tu serais    | tu irais    | tu ferais    |
| Il, Elle, On | il aurait    | il serait    | il irait    | il ferait    |
| Nous         | nous aurions | nous serions | nous irions | nous ferions |
| Vous         | vous auriez  | vous seriez  | vous iriez  | vous feriez  |
| Ils, Elles   | ils auraient | ils seraient | ils iraient | ils feraient |

3. Exceptions

> The exceptions in the conditionnel présent are the same as those in the `futur simple.`

1. Add a grave accent to an é or e that appears in the final syllable of the verb stem

   - peser -> je pèserais
   - acheter -> j'achèterais
   - modeler -> je modèlerais

2. Verbs ending in -eler or -eter double their final consonant before adding the endings

   - jeter -> je jetterais
   - épeler -> j'épellerais

3. Verbs ending in -yer, y becomes i.

   - employer -> j'emploierais, tu emploierais, il emploierait, nous emploierions, vous emploieriez, ils emploieraient
     `But` For verbs ending in ayer, both y and u are allowed. (Exception: envoyer) -> j'enverrais and renvoyer -> je renverrais)
   - payer -> je payerais/paierais

4. The i certain -rir verbs disappears when we add the endings

   - courir -> je courrais, tu courrais, il courrait, nous courrions, vous courriez, ils courraient
   - mourir -> je mourrais, tu mourrais, il mourrait, nous mourrions, vous mourriez, ils mourraient

5. Verbs ending in -oir, as well as aller, envoyer, falloir and venir are irregular.

   - [Full List of irregular verbs](https://francais.lingolia.com/en/grammar/tenses/irregular-verbs)
   - pouvoir -> je pourrais, tu pourrais, il pourrait, nous pourrions, vous pourriez, ils pourraient

#### Examples

- Je voudrais = I would like
- J'avais = I would have

### Le Imparfait: Imperfect Tense

- [Link](https://francais.lingolia.com/en/grammar/tenses/imparfait)

> L’imparfait (the imperfect) is a French past tense. It describes states and actions that were ongoing or repeated in the past.

- The imperfect can correspond to the English simple past tense (I did, I went …)
- But also to structures such as used to and would and even the past progressive (I was doing, I was going …)
- L’imparfait is used to tell stories and report on past actions, mostly in written contexts.
- We conjugate the imperfect by adding the endings -ais, -ais, -ait, -ions, -iez and -aient to the root of the present tense nous form of the verb.

### How to conjugate the imperfect tense in French

To conjugate the imperfect tense in French, we take the present-tense stem of the 1st person plural form of the verb (the nous form), and add the following endings (identical for verbs of all groups):

| Personne   | -er verbs    | -ir verbs (nous finissons)\* | -ir verbs (nous doemons)\*\* | -re verbs     |
| ---------- | ------------ | ---------------------------- | ---------------------------- | ------------- |
| je         | j'aimais     | je finissais                 | je dormais                   | je vendais    |
| tu         | tu aimais    | tu finissais                 | tu dormais                   | tu vendais    |
| il/elle/on | il aimait    | il finissait                 | il dormait                   | il vendait    |
| nous       | nous aimions | nous finissions              | nous dormions                | nous vendions |
| vous       | vous aimiez  | vous finissiez               | vous dormiez                 | vous vendiez  |
| ils/elles  | ils aimaient | ils finissaient              | ils dormaient                | ils vendaient |

The verb avoir is regular in the imperfect; only être has an irregular conjugation in this tense.

| Personne   | avoir   | etre    | faire     | aller    |
| ---------- | ------- | ------- | --------- | -------- |
| je         | avais   | étais   | faisais   | allais   |
| tu         | avais   | étais   | faisais   | allais   |
| il/elle/on | avait   | était   | faisait   | allait   |
| nous       | avions  | étions  | faisions  | allions  |
| vous       | aviez   | étiez   | faisiez   | alliez   |
| ils/elles  | avaient | étaient | faisaient | allaient |

#### When to use `l'imparfait`

1.  To describe a situation in the past
    - Ex: Quand il `était` petit, Lucas `aimait` beaucoup les gâteaux. = When he was little, Lucas loved cakes.
2.  To describe a person, a property, a comment or an explanation in the past
    - Ex: Lucas `était` un enfant très gourmand. = Lucas was a very greedy child.
3.  To describe a repeated action in the past
    - Ex: Tous les jours, il `faisait` un gâteau avec sa grand-mère. = Every day, he made a cake with his grandmother.
4.  To talk about simultaneously occurring actions in the past
    - Ex: Lucas `mangeait` un gâteau pendant que sa grand-mère `préparait` le repas. = Lucas was eating a cake while his grandmother was preparing the meal.
    - Ex: Pendant quíl `regardait` la recette, Florence `préparait` la pâte. = While he was looking at the recipe, Florence was preparing the dough.
5.  To emphasize the duration of an action
    - Ex: Ils `cuisinaient` le gâteau puis `mangeaient` une part au dessert. = They cooked the cake and then ate a piece for dessert.
